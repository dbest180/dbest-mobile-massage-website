Swedish Massage

The classic relaxation massage for stress relief and overall wellness

Duration: 60 or 90 minutes
Pressure: Light to Medium
Best For: Relaxation, Stress Relief

What is Swedish Massage?

Swedish massage is the most common type of massage therapy, focusing on relaxation and overall wellness. Using long, flowing strokes, kneading, and circular movements, this technique promotes relaxation, improves circulation, and relieves muscle tension.

Key Benefits:

  • Reduces stress and promotes deep relaxation
  • Improves blood circulation throughout the body
  • Relieves muscle tension and stiffness
  • Enhances flexibility and range of motion
  • Boosts immune system function
  • Improves skin tone and complexion

Techniques Used:

Effleurage

Long, gliding strokes to warm up muscle tissue

Petrissage

Kneading and lifting movements to release tension

Tapotement

Rhythmic tapping to invigorate and stimulate

Friction

Deep circular movements to break up adhesions

Deep Tissue Massage

Targeted therapy for chronic pain and deep muscle tension

Duration: 60 or 90 minutes
Pressure: Medium to Deep
Best For: Chronic Pain, Injury Recovery

What is Deep Tissue Massage?

Deep tissue massage targets the deeper layers of muscles and connective tissue. This therapeutic approach uses slower strokes and deeper pressure to release chronic muscle tension, break up scar tissue, and address specific problem areas.

Key Benefits:

  • Breaks up and eliminates scar tissue
  • Reduces chronic muscle pain and tension
  • Improves range of motion and flexibility
  • Helps with injury rehabilitation
  • Reduces inflammation and muscle spasms
  • Improves posture and body alignment

Ideal For These Conditions:

Chronic back pain
Limited mobility
Recovering from injuries
Muscle tension in neck and shoulders
Repetitive strain injuries
Postural problems
Note: Some soreness is normal after deep tissue massage as your muscles release stored tension. This typically subsides within 24-48 hours.

What is Hot Stone Therapy?

Hot stone massage incorporates smooth, heated basalt stones placed on specific points of the body and used as massage tools. The combination of heat and therapeutic massage creates a deeply relaxing experience that melts away tension.

Key Benefits:

  • Alleviates stress and promotes mental calmness
  • Reduces pain and inflammation

  • Promotes better sleep and overall wellness

Sports Massage

Performance enhancement and injury prevention for athletes

Duration: 60 or 90 minutes
Focus: Performance & Recovery
Best For: Athletes, Active Individuals

What is Sports Massage?

Sports massage is specifically designed for people who are active, whether professionally or recreationally. This technique focuses on preventing and treating injuries, enhancing performance, and helping with recovery from strenuous physical activity.

Key Benefits:

  • Prevents injuries by identifying potential problem areas
  • Enhances athletic performance and flexibility
  • Reduces recovery time between workouts
  • Addresses sport-specific muscle groups and patterns
  • Breaks down adhesions and scar tissue
  • Improves range of motion and muscle efficiency

When to Get Sports Massage:

Pre-Event

Light, stimulating massage 1-2 days before competition to prepare muscles

Post-Event

Recovery-focused massage within 48 hours after competition

Maintenance

Regular sessions during training to prevent injuries and maintain performance

Rehabilitation

Targeted work on injured areas to restore function and mobility

Enhance Your Performance

Whether you're training for competition or staying active, sports massage can help you perform at your best.
